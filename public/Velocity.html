    The goal of this project was to design a mobile trading app for the Thai market.

    My role

    UX, UI, and research.

Background

Velocity is a project I worked on during my internship at a design agency in Bangkok.

Mission

The main scope was to develop a mobile trading app for the Thai market. Through Velocity users can earn capital and trade stocks in a safe enviroment. Velocity makes it possible to trade, sell and buy stocks at any time for anyone.

Phase 1 - define & understand

We initiated the project by exploring the problem space and defining the problem.
I also wrote a project brief and formed a timeplan for the project.

The problem

The threshold for entering the stock market is too high for most Thais. Only about 3% of the country participates in the stock market. We want to increase that number by creating an app that makes stock investment easier for Thais. Key problems are :

  1. Don't have disposible income for trading.
  2. Risk averse.
  3. Lack of knowledge of the stock market.
  4. Financial insecurity and tumultous economy.
"How can we make trading more accessible for local Thais"

Phase 2 - research

After understanding the problem I conducted neccessary research to understand who our users are and their goals.

Competitor analysis

A competitor analysis was made to find market gaps, current solutions, and weak points. For the analysis, I found a direct and indirect competitor.

Target audience

Understanding market demographics is key so I conducted research into market demographics and the current state of retail investors in Thailand. This is what I found:

  1. Millennials account for more than half of retail investors and Gen Z accounts for 24%
  2. Across both generations over the internet is the preferred way to trade.
  3. Millennials are risk averse compared to Gen Z. Financial insecurity is high across millennals
    and Gen Z in Bangkok due to rising cost of living and tumultous economy.

With these, in mind, I constructed two proto- personas based on the millennial day trader and the newer Gen z trader.

Notes

  1. Lack of knowledge seems to be the biggest challenge.
    Most Thais don't partake in trading because they lack knowledge of the stock market and view it as risky.
  2. I used ​​​"The 2021 Household Socio-Economic Survey" from the National Statistical Office.
    The Stock Exchange of Thailand has yearly papers with market statistics and demographics that I used.

Phase 3 - ideation

After establishing the problem and carving out potential users I began to work on the design of the app and the user journey.

Userflows and paper wireframes.

I mapped out the order in which users would complete key tasks like purchasing or selling a stock. This allowed me to understand the steps users took and the features needed to complete their tasks.
After a flow was constructed I began drawing paper wireframes by the flow which I, unfortunately, do not have photos for.

Phase 4 - prototyping

A prototype was made in Figma. Due to the time constraints i was able to only complete two screens.

Homescreen

The user is greeted by current positions and balance. I opted to move the secondary navigation to the top. I received feedback that the navigation cut off the screen weirdly in the middle and was distracting. I also decided to add more information in the form of cards showing current indexes. A graph was added to showcase user balance journey/history.

Stock screen

The stock screen remains mostly unchanged. The user is greeted by current positions and numbers.
This screen was the biggest challenge for me as I had to cram in so much information onto one screen
while still keeping everything legible and not overwhelm the user.

Results

  1. By the end of my internship, all of the work and resources were handed over to my supervisors and they are currently working on this project. If I had more time the next step would have been to fully complete the prototype in Figma and then conduct usability tests.
  2. The lack of user and stakeholder involvement was an issue for me throughout the project. I had to hypothesize and come to a lot of conclusions on my own which was difficult. If I could I would have liked to have interviews and some usability testing of wireframes.
  3. Overall though as my first ever real project I learned a lot from this project and am grateful to my supervisors for taking me in and supporting me!
